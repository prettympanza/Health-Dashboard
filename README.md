# Sexually Transmitted Disease Dashboard

---

# Overview
This project presents an interactive dashboard designed to analyse and visualise trends in sexually transmitted diseases (STDs). It highlights patterns in new cases, deaths, prevalence, and incidence across different regions.

A key feature of the dashboard is the slicer, which allows users to dynamically switch between diseases and compare their behaviour over time and geography.

---

# Features

## Disease Selection
Users can filter the dashboard using a slicer to view data for:
- Hepatitis B  
- HIV/AIDS  
- Human Papillomavirus (HPV)  
- Syphilis  

## Time-Based Analysis
- Tracks new cases and deaths over time  
- Enables identification of trends and changes in disease progression  

## Regional Analysis
- Prevalence rates across regions  
- Incidence rates across regions  
- Highlights geographic differences and potential high-risk areas  

## Global Distribution
- Provides a geographic perspective of disease spread

# Key Insights

---

## Overall Trends
- Across all diseases shown, both new cases and deaths decline over time, indicating either improved treatment, awareness, or reporting changes.
- Death counts consistently remain lower than new cases but follow similar downward trends.

---

## Regional Patterns

### Africa
- Shows the highest prevalence for Hepatitis B and remains among the highest for other diseases.
- Incidence rates are consistently high, suggesting ongoing transmission challenges.

### Europe
- Displays lower prevalence for Hepatitis B but rises significantly for HPV.
- Indicates variation in disease burden depending on the condition.

### Asia
- Moderate to high prevalence across diseases.
- Strong presence on the global distribution map, suggesting large population impact.

### Americas
- Generally, mid-range prevalence and incidence across all diseases.
- More stable compared to other regions.

### Oceania
- Typically shows the lowest prevalence, but incidence remains comparable to other regions in some cases.

---

## Disease-Specific Observations

### Hepatitis B
- Highest prevalence in Africa, with a noticeable gap compared to other regions.
- Clear downward trend in both cases and deaths over time.

### HIV/AIDS
- (From previous dashboard view)
- Strong presence in Africa, aligning with known global patterns.
- Gradual decline in cases and deaths.

### HPV
- Europe and Africa show the highest prevalence rates.
- Asia records the lowest prevalence among regions.
- Cases and deaths show a steady decline over time.

---

## Global Distribution
- The maps indicate higher disease concentration in parts of Africa and Asia.
- Europe and North America generally show lighter intensity, depending on the disease selected.

---

## Interpretation
- There is a clear geographical disparity in disease burden.
- Regions with higher prevalence also tend to have higher incidence, suggesting persistent transmission cycles.
- The consistent downward trends may reflect improvements in healthcare, vaccination, and awareness programmes.
